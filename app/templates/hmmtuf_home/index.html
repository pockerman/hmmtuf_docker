About

Thermodynamically ultra-fastened (TUF) regions are stretches of the DNA which fail to denature even after the application of extreme melting conditions. This behavior effectively reduces the amplification efficiency in these regions. It has also been reported that TUF regions contain a core sequence which exhibits an increased GC concentration relative to the surrounding DNA. It is in fact these locally concentrated spikes of GC content which is believed to remain duplexed despite the application of denaturation processes.

HMMtuf is an simple mapping tool that uses hidden Markov models in order to label regions of DNA. It was developed at the University of Leicester, UK. Users can load their own developed models or create new ones. Users can load their own DNA region data for modelling.

The application is free of charge and can be downloaded from the project's GitHub repository here.
